Research

My research bridges machine learning and decision sciences, developing robust, interpretable models for high-stakes applications. Drawing from metaheuristic optimization and MCDM, I focus on predictive systems that deliver actionable insights in finance and healthcare.

With expertise in processing MRI/CT scans, microarray genomics data, and evolutionary algorithms, I design lightweight neural architectures for edge deployment and real-time diagnostics.

Publications

Journal Articles (5)

  1. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2025). Developing a two stage optimized random vector functional link neural network based predictor model utilizing a swift crow search algorithm. Cluster Computing, 28(3), 161. DOI

    Summary: A two‑step forecasting setup: first pick the best features, then tune the RVFL neural net using a Swift Crow Search Algorithm, an improved variant of the original CSA. Applied to stock indices, it improves both price and direction predictions compared to baselines.

  2. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2023). Developing a novel stock index trend predictor model by integrating multiple criteria decision-making with an optimized online sequential extreme learning machine. Granular Computing, 8(3), 411–440. DOI

    Summary: Focuses on predicting trend (up/down) based of predicted Price of a stock index using an OSELM-MCDM framework that learns online. Handy for streaming data where quick trend signals matter more than exact prices.

  3. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2022). A Novel MCDM Ensemble Approach of Designing an ELM-Based Predictor for Stock Index Price Forecasting. Intelligent Decision Technologies, 16(2), 387–406. DOI

    Summary: Builds an ensemble of MCDM-ELM models where a Occurrence-based Rank-Sum ensemble of MCDM decides which models to include and how to weight them. The ensemble beats single ELMs and offers more stable forecasts across different market conditions.

  4. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2021). A TOPSIS-ELM framework for stock index price movement prediction. Intelligent Decision Technologies, 15(2), 201–220. DOI

    Summary: An earlier experimentation pairing TOPSIS (a decision tool) with ELM to pick the best ELM-Activation function configurations for binary up/down prediction. Useful if you want a systematic way to choose activation function for ELM model.

  5. Dash, R., Samal, S., Dash, R., & Rautray, R. (2019). An integrated TOPSIS crow search-based classifier ensemble: In application to stock index price movement prediction. Applied Soft Computing, 85, 105784. DOI

    Summary: Combines TOPSIS to rank classifiers and Crow Search to tune ensemble weights. On several indices, this hybrid ensemble beats plain majority voting and single classifiers in stock index movement prediction.

Conference Proceedings (5)

  1. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2022). Stock Index Movement Prediction: A Crow Search-ELM Approach. In Innovation in Electrical Power Engineering, Communication, and Computing Technology, pp. 349–356. Springer, Singapore. DOI

    Summary: Applies Crow Search Algorithm to tune ELM for movement prediction. A compact, practical demo of meta‑heuristic + fast learner for financial forecasting tasks.

  2. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2022). An Empirical Comparison of TOPSIS and VIKOR for Ranking Decision-Making Models. In Intelligent and Cloud Computing, pp. 429–437. Springer, Singapore. DOI

    Summary: Compares two popular MCDM methods (TOPSIS vs VIKOR) for ranking benchmark models. Handy guidance when you need to pick a ranking method for model selection.

  3. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2021). A TWV Classifier Ensemble Framework. In Intelligent and Cloud Computing, 255–262. Springer, Singapore. DOI

    Summary: Presents a TOPSIS-based Weighted Voting ensemble (classifier combination framework) focused on improving robustness in index movement prediction.

  4. Samal, S., & Dash, R. (2021). A Hybrid Fruit Fly ELM Framework for Stock Index Price Movement Prediction. In 2021 International Conference on Advances in Power, Signal, and Information Technology (APSIT), pp. 1–5. IEEE. DOI

    Summary: Combines the Fruit‑Fly optimization algorithm with ELM to tune model parameters for binary movement prediction. A quick, meta‑heuristic based tuning approach that worked well in experiments.

  5. Dash, R., Samal, S., Rautray, R., & Dash, R. (2019). A TOPSIS approach of ranking classifiers for stock index price movement prediction. In Soft Computing in Data Analytics, pp. 665–674. Springer, Singapore. DOI

    Summary: Early work that uses TOPSIS to rank classifiers (RBF, SVM, kNN, etc.) for stock index movement prediction. A principled way to compare base learners beyond single performance evaluation metrics.

Biography

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at SRM University-AP. My research focuses on predictive modeling, hybrid neural networks, and metaheuristic optimization for applications in financial forecasting and healthcare diagnostics.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science & Engineering from Siksha 'O' Anusandhan (Deemed to be University), Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India (2025), where I developed optimized Lightweight Neural Networks using MCDM and Evolutionary Optimization algorithms.
